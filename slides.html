The Pulley Ridge Project
  • 2012 -2018 NOAA funded project
  • Big Science - 20+ PIs
  • Interdisciplinary - Biology, Physical Oceanography, Economics, Genetics, Ecology, Marine Science
  • At outset NOAA wanted to experiment with "data Curation"
  • Center of Computer Science invited as:
    • data curators
    • creators of decision support tool

  • Lessons Learned

Goals of Project
  • Curate the data collection as it emerges from the project
  • Allows for synthesis between researchers and disciplines
  • Seek connections not visible from one disciplinary perspective
  • Share the information with decision makers (resource managers)
  • Make information available to general public
Encountered Success
  • Curated collection of metadata
  • Platform for discovery of data??
  • Search engine / data shopping?? (SOLR as indexer)
  • Visualization (chart js) and Cartography (leaflet) as Bridge??
  • Collaboration between Library and CCS (DOIs and metadata)
Desperate Failure
  • Sharing of data - not!
  • Storytelling and synthesis (EIGERT and NSF)
  • Lack of designers on project
  • Search engine / metadata
  • Data Discovery
Lessons Learned
  • Metadata
  • Cartography online - Collaborations with Researchers
  • Collaboration NOAA (NCEI), CCS, Library
  • Institutional Knowledge (SOLR, metadata)
Open Questions
  • Assume that goals are good
  • use community built tool or build your own?
  • OAI-PMH in the tool - real discoverability??
  • Map systems vs. cartography
  • Who synthesizes? Who tells the story?
Conclusions
  • Up front expectations need to be set - policy!!
  • For those involved it was a research experience - exploration of possibility
  • It was all about re-use and storytelling - metadata creation adds value to both
  • Many failures, but no time lost
